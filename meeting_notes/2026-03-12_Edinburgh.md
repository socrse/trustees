 ## Topics discussed

- Reserves policy needs to be updated, requires some analysis of how much we need. Options for distribution of accumulated surplus once we have a final figure for the reserves policy were discussed. 
- Discussion on whether we should record protected characteristics in our membership system to determine how diverse our membership is. 
  - Expect that getting existing members to fill in would be difficult
  - We have information from other surveys and RSECon, decided not to record this data
- The Community Survey results were discussed
  - Large part of motivation is that people want to be part of a community
  - Bundling membership with conference tickets may not be possible with tax implications
  - SocRSE should have better comms on what the benefits of membership are
  - There was interest in career development options, accreditation and training provision
- How do we improve out engagement with RSEs outside of university/academia
  - Our website and a lot of public facing comms has an academic focus, want to make sure that we aren't excluding RSEs
  - About 15% of survey members working outside academic institutions
  - We are keen to continue and expand engagement with industry-based RSEs, advantage for our membership to foster more collaborations between industry and academia, and for career mobility. 
- Ethical sponsorship policy vote outcomes
  - We will update the terms of delegation for the conference to reference the process and increase amount of trustee consultation for sponsors
  - Make an internal document to log decisions about trustee consultation on sponsorship for future trustees
  - Current list of sponsorship options shared with trustees for review
- Recruiting members for the new Policy and Funding working group
